Rationale and Background

  1. Rationale

The Residential Property Price Index (RPPI) is a vital metric that monitors fluctuations in residential property prices over time, offering valuable insights to various stakeholders in the real estate market, including homeowners, investors, policymakers, and analysts. For investors, the RPPI serves as a crucial tool for making well-informed decisions by identifying potential growth areas and optimal investment timings. Policymakers heavily rely on RPPI data to craft effective housing and economic policies that promote housing affordability and stability in the market. Additionally, the RPPI aids in identifying market trends and provides valuable guidance in formulating measures to ensure a stable and affordable housing market.

  1. Background

The Residential Property Price Index is compiled from diverse sources, including real estate transactions, listings, and surveys. It utilizes a robust methodology, considering property types, locations, and relevant factors.

  • Data Collection: This RPPI data is sourced from web scraping, covering apartments for sale and houses for sale.
  • Methodology: RPPI is calculated using hedonic regression, considering property characteristics for price changes.
  • Updates: RPPI is regularly updated monthly to reflect the latest market developments.
  • Coverage: The index includes properties mainly in Kigali city, offering a comprehensive view of price movements at regional levels.
